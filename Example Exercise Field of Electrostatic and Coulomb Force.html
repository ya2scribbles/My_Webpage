Example Exercise Field of Electrostatic and Coulomb Force

Exercise that student get in highschool according to this subchapter could be broken down into basic, equilibrium condition, parallel vector, two vector with angle, and more than two vector. For concept and knowledge question, we will not talk about it in here.

Basic

  1. Two charge with magnitude -8 μC and 12 μC separate with distance 4 m. Calculate Coulomb force that affect these charge!
    2. qA = -8 μC = -8 x 10-6 C \(F_{AB}=\frac{k q_Aq_B}{r^2}\)
    qB = 12 μC = 12 x 10-6 C \(F_{AB} = \frac{9 \times 10^9 \times 8 \times 10^{-6} \times 12 \times 10^{-6}}{4^2}\)
    R = 4 m \( \mathbf{F_{AB} = 54 \times 10^{-3} N}\)
    FAB = ......? \( \mathbf{F_{AB} = 0,054 N}\)
  2. What is magnitude of electrostatic field 4 cm from +16 μC Charge?
    3. Q = +16 μC = +16 x 10-6 C \(E = k \frac{Q}{r^2}\)
    r = 4 cm = 4 x 10-2 m \(E = 9 \times 10^9 \frac{16 \times 10^{-6}}{{(4 \times 10^{-2}})^2}\)
    E = ......? \( \mathbf{E = 9 \times 10^7 N/C}\)
  3. If the same value of charge separate 10 cm feel attractive force around 160 N, find the value of those charge!
    4. qA = qB = ......? \(F_{AB}=\frac{k q_Aq_B}{r^2}\) \( \sqrt{\frac{160}{9 \times 10^{11}}} = q \)
    r = 10 cm = 10 x 10-2 m \(160 = 9 \times 10^9 \frac{q^2}{(10 \times 10^{-2})^2}\) \( \mathbf{\frac{4}{3} \times 10^{-5} C = q} \)
    FAB = 160 N \( \frac{160}{9 \times 10^{11}} = q^2 \) \( \mathbf{1,333 \times 10^{-5}} \)
  4. If one point feel electrostatic field is 216 N/C, find distance of that point to the -40 μC charge!
    5. E = 216 N/C \(E = k \frac{Q}{r^2}\) \( r^2 = \frac{36}{216} \times 10^4 \)
    Q = -40 μC = -40 x 10-6 C \(216 = 9 \times 10^9 \frac{40 \times 10^{-6}}{r^2}\) \( r = \sqrt{\frac{36}{216} \times 10^4} \)
    r = ......? \( r^2 = 9 \times 10^9 \frac{40 \times 10^{-6}}{216} \) \( \mathbf{r = 37,5 m} \)
  5. Two charge separate around 12 cm. if they feel repel force 324 N and one of charge is 12 μC, what is another charge?
    6. r = 12 cm = 12 x 10-2 m \(F_{AB}=\frac{k q_Aq_B}{r^2}\)
    FAB = 324 N \(324 = 9 \times 10^9 \frac{12 \times 10^{-6} \times q_B}{(12 \times 10^{-2})^2}\)
    qA = 12 μC = 12 x 10-6 C \( \frac{324}{9 \times 10^7} = q_B \)
    qB = ......? \( \mathbf{36 \times 10^{-7}=q_B} \)

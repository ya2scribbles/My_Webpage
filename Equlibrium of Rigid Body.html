Concept of Torque

Torque is a quantity that make thing rotate. It’s occurs when force applied to lever arm of things, hence that things rotated according to the axis of rotation. That’s why torque can be said as the result of cross product between force and radius. This quantity explain why it’s so easy lift load using lever. Interaction between force and lever arm or radius could be seen in this picture :

As we can see in the picture, torque occurs when force perpendicular with lever arm/radius. Hence, for torque we have equation :

τ⃗=r⃗×F⃗ or τ=F.r

When force and lever arm/radius not perpendicular (or making an angle), then we take the projection which perpendicular with either force or radius. Meanwhile, if the direction of force and lever arm/ radius parallel that’s mean there is no torque in that system. That’s why if there is a vector force and vector radius, then we could find torque vector using cross product. To understand concept of torque, please take a look of example below.


In this picture we have example of torque with four perpendicular, five acute, and three obtuse force with lever arm. take a look case number one, as we can see the lever arm and force already perpendicular of each other. Hence the torque that work on this case is (24x22) 528 N.m. same through number four. Meanwhile for number five, we can see the lever arm is not perpendicular with the force. Hence for every case when it happen, it's better to make projection in front of angle on sloping side according to horizontal or vertical side. Then, we choose the projection which perpendicular with vertical or horizontal side.

If the perpendicular side in opposite of angle, then we multiple it with sin of an angle. Meanwhile, if the perpendicular side in adjecent of angle, then we multiple it with cos of an angle. You could see the projection of sloping side of number five until twelve in this picture below :


in this picture, we already make projection of sloping side on prospective horizontal or vertical side. To make it easy to understand, the projection we use to calculate torque is the green one because it’s the projection that perpendicular to horizontal or vertical side. Meanwhile we don’t use the red one because this projection parallel to horizontal or vertical side. Take example number 5, we have green line in opposite of angle. Hence, the value of torque this example is 25 x 30 x sin (37) = 450 N.m. For example number ten, the green line at the adjecent of angle. Hence, the value of torque this example is 40 x 20 x cos 53 = 480 N.m. that's example of basic torque. this example made for make sure student really grasp the concept of Torque.


Resultant of Torque

In the reality, the torque that affect thing can be more than one. Hence we need to calculate the total or resultant of this torque. Usually we assign torque that rotate counterclockwise as positive and torque that rotate clockwise as negative. We can see it in this picture :

If we have more than one torque that work in one thing, that means we need to find resultant of torque. The resultant of torque would determine either things rotate counter-clockwise/clockwise or rest. If the body in the rest position, that’s mean every resultant torque and force that act in that thing is equal to zero. Hence this the thing that engineer want when construct building, with the hope building stand still in the long time.


Take example from the right picture. We have plank with axis on the left. And there’s six force act on this plank. Hence, we can conclude there’s six torque. Start with force close with the axis, we will determined value of each torque. Force 40 N act on axis, hence there is no torque here : τ1 = 0 N.m. Second torque 60 N at angle 37O on 0,5 m from axis : τ2 = 60x0,5xsin37O = 18 N.m. Third torque 25 N at angle 30O on 1 m from axis : τ3 = 25x1xsin30O = 12,5 N.m. Fourth torque 30 N at angle 53O on 1,5 m (1+0,5) from axis : τ4 = 30x1,5xsin53O = 60 N.m. Fifth torque 42 N already perpendicular on 2 m (1+0,5+0,5) from axis : τ5 = 42x2 = 84 N.m. Sixth torque 36 N already perpendicular on 3 m (1+0,5+0,5+1) from axis : τ6 = 36x3 = 108 N.m. After that, determine which torque counter-clockwise and clockwise before sum it all. From the picture, we could see from picture torque 2, 4, 6 rotate counter-clockwise hence it’s positive. Meanwhile torque 1, 3, 5 rotate clockwise hence it’s negative. Finally, we find resultant torque : Στ = -τ1+τ2-τ3+τ4-τ5+τ6 = -0+18-12,5+60-84+108 = 89,5 N.m.

In real life, the thing that applied torque not only plank. Everything could be affected by it. That’s why in engineering course, we will learn more deep about it. But for now, we only stick with line (plank), square/ rectangle, and circle.

Example from figure above give you resultant torque not zero, hence the object above will be rotate counter-clockwise or clockwise. When resultant of torque equal zero, that’s mean object does not rotate. Hence this is the next thing we will learn. Next we will learn about equilibrium of rigid body.


Equilibrium of Rigid Body

Equilibrium of rigid body achieved by net zero on resultant of torque and resultant of force. That’s why when we try to analyze equilibrium of Rigid body, there are three things that we need consider:

  • First, net force on x component must be zero.
  • Second, net force on y component must be zero.
  • Third, net torque must be zero.

When you are study this in high school level, there are around five type problem for equilibrium of rigid body. I would say these problem can be divide into :

  • Bench in the park
  • Advertisement board
  • Ladder leaning against wall
  • Ball meet stairs/box
  • seesaw/lever

Those are five type of problem that usually high school student encounter when they learn equilibrium of rigid body.

Usually for problem bench at the park, the question is to find value of each normal force. Analyzing force and torque, we would find the equation like this :

Στ=0, take left axis. ΣFx=0 ΣFy=0
Στ=wb.l1+wp.(l1+l2)-N2.(l1+l2+l3)
0=wb.l1+wp.(l1+l2)-N2.(l1+l2+l3)
N2.(l1+l2+l3)=wb.l1+wp.(l1+l2)		 No force at x direction. ΣFy=N1+N2-wb-wp
0=N1+N2-wb-wp
wb+wp=N1+N2

That’s equation that we get from free diagram according to example on the figure. Meanwhile for variety problem that’s close enough with this type, you need to draw free diagram first before continue with analyzing torque and force. After finishing analyzing, work on to answer question that problem want. Use math as a tool to find the answer which exercise want.

Usually for problem advertisement board, the question is to determined value of string tension, magnitude of contact force between block and wall. As we can see in figure on the left, there are many force that need to analyze. For example of this type of problem, we use board sign that hanging in the end of block which is lean to wall and pull by string to the wall. Hence, from this we already know there will be 2 type of weight, two type of tension force, and two type of force that act at wall. If we take a closer look, contact surface between block and wall is smooth. That’s mean there is no friction happen between this two surface. And value of T1 equals with wp.

Analyzing force and torque, we would find the equation like this :

Στ=0, take wall as axis. ΣFx=0 ΣFy=0
Στ=wb.0,5.l.cos(θ1)+T1.l.cos(θ1)-T2.l.sin(θ1+θ2)
0=wb.0,5.l.cos(θ1)+T1.l.cos(θ1)-T2.l.sin(θ1+θ2)
T2.l.sin(θ1+θ2)=wb.0,5.l.cos(θ1)+T1.l.cos(θ1)		 Fx=H-T2cos(θ2)
0=H-T2cos(θ2)
H=T2cos(θ2)		 ΣFy=V+T2.sin(θ2)-wb-wp
0=V+T2.sin(θ2)-wb-wp
V=wb+wp-T2.sin(θ2)

That’s equation that we get from free diagram according to example on the figure. Meanwhile for variety problem that’s close enough with this type, you need to draw free diagram first before continue with analyzing torque and force. Because there are still more example of exercise that using this type. After finishing analyzing, work on to answer question that problem want. Use math as a tool to find the answer which exercise want.

Usually for problem ladder leaning against wall, the question is to determined how long person could step up before ladder slipping, coefficient of friction between ladder and floor, or magnitude of normal force. As we can see in figure on the left, there are many force that need to analyze. For example of this type of problem, we have condition where ladder leaning against wall and kid try to step up the ladder with floor and ladder have rough surface and wall and ladder have smooth surface (there is no friction). Hence, from this we already know there will be 2 type of weight, two type of normal force, and one static friction force between floor and ladder. For some exercise they will give you distance from the wall and length of ladder, meanwhile another exercise only give you length of ladder and angle at ladder and floor.

Analyzing force and torque, we would find the equation like this :

Στ=0, take floor as axis. ΣFx=0 ΣFy=0
Στ=wl.0,5.l.cos(θ)+wp.l.wp.cos(θ)-N2.l.sin(θ)
0=wl.0,5.l.cos(θ)+wp.l.wp.cos(θ)-N2.l.sin(θ)
N2.l.sin(θ)=wl.0,5.l.cos(θ)+wp.l.wp.cos(θ)		 Fx=N2-fs
0=N2-fs
N2=fs		 ΣFy=N1-wl-wp
0=N1-wl-wp
N1=wl+wp

That’s equation that we get from free diagram according to example on the figure. Meanwhile for variety problem that’s close enough with this type, you need to draw free diagram first before continue with analyzing torque and force. After finishing analyzing, work on to answer question that problem want. Use math as a tool to find the answer which exercise want.

Usually for problem ball meets stair/box, the question is to determined how much force that we need to lift ball to the stair or box. As we can see in figure on the left, there are many force that need to analyze. For example of this type of problem, we have condition where ball step up using force at the center of mass ball and contact force between stair/box and ball. Hence, from this picture we work with weight of ball, force given at the center of mass, and contact force between ball and stair/box.

Analyzing force and torque, we would find the equation like this :

Στ=0, take contact point as axis. ΣFx=0 ΣFy=0
Στ=F.R.cos(θ)-wb.R.sin(θ)
0=F.R.cos(θ)-wb.R.sin(θ)
F.R.cos(θ)=wb.R.sin(θ)		 Fx=F-Nx
0=F-Nx
F=Nx		 ΣFy=Ny-wb
0=Ny-wb
Ny=wb

As we can see, when we try to solve torque equation we will face trigonometry. For this case, i put the angle at the center of ball (radius and weight). To find the value of trigonometry from this angle, we could use Pythagorean theorem. For hypotenuse is R, adjacent of this is (R-h), lastly opposite side is (R2-(R-h)2)1/2. Then, we could solve torque equation.

Usually for problem seesaw/lever, the question is to determined how much force that we need to balance the seesaw or lever. As we can see in figure on the above, there are many force that need to analyze. For example of this type of problem, we have condition where lever must be in the condition balance. Hence, from the picture we have three force to work in on and 2 torque to balance seesaw with fulcrum as axis.

Analyzing force and torque, we would find the equation like this :

Στ=0, take fulcrum as axis. ΣFx=0 ΣFy=0
Στ=w.l1-F.l2
0=w.l1-F.l2
F.l2=w.l1		 No force at x direction. ΣFy=N-w+F
0=N-w+F
w=N+F

From this equation, we can find the force that we need to balance the seesaw and find magnitude of normal force which work on fulcrum.

These all the majority problem that student will find in the exercise of equilibrium of rigid body. The key is to get used doing exercise and develop sense of problem solving to get a gist solution of analysis task.

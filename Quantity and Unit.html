Base and Derived Quantity

Physics quantity is defined as attribute to base properties of phenomenon of nature which has quantitative meaning. Physics quantity base on indeendency divide into base quantity and derived quantity. Base quatity is physical quantity which cannot explain or elaborate become more simple. Hence it's buildong block of every derived quantity. From that, we can understand that derived quantity is physical quantity which derived from base quantity. Hence, it's existence can be take a root from base quantity. Every quantity could be measure using a instrument and get valued next with their units. Derived Quantity whose has same of component base quantity units is known as Equivalent Quantity.

Units is standard amount of a physical quantity, defined and adopted by convention, against which measurements of that physical quantity are made. In physics, units divede into SI unit and non SI unit. SI is the metric system or international standars that is used universally as a standard for measurements. Meamwhile a non SI units are units from another standardized which not up to international standard. Usually the name of SI Units could be different with to composition of it's base quantity. Hence we could say that these Units whose has the same of base quantity units are Equivalent.

According to need of direction, physical quantity divide into scalar and vector quantity. Scalar quantity are physical quantity that only contain magnitude (example: distance, speed, temperature, work, momen inertia, mass, etc). Meanwhile Vector quantity are physical quantity that contain magnitude and direction (example: displacement, velocity, acceleration, torque, force, etc).

Metric prefix is unit prefect which precedes a basic unit of measure to indicate multiple/submultiple of the units. Each prefix has a unique symbol that is prepended to any unit symbol. For example prefix kilo could be add into metre, hence it become kilogram which is equivalent with one thousand gram.

Hence, if you want to convert units to another prefix, you just need substract the power of ten on each prefix and multiplied it with value of unit. And if there is no prefix, it's 100. Take a look of this example:

  • 50 second = ...... milisecond → second to milisecond (100-(-3)=103),hence 50.103 milisecond or 50.000 milisecond.
  • 400 kb = ...... Mb → kb to Mb (103-6=10-3), hence 400.10-3 Mb or 0,4 Mb.
  • 3 km = ...... cm → km to cm (103-(-2)=105), hence 3.105 cm or 300000 cm.
  • 0,2 hg = ...... Gg → hg to Gg (102-9=10-7), hence 0,2.10-7 Gg or 0,00000002 Gg.
  • 0,001 mA = ...... nA → mA to nA (103-(-9)=1012), hence 0,001.1012 nA or 0,000000002 nA.

To convert the value from units left to the right, just multiplied with left value. Meanwhile, for convert left units to right units, just divided with left value. See these example:

  • 4 minute = ...... second → minute to second (4x60 = 240), hence 240 second.
  • 9 Bar = ...... mmHg → Bar to mmHg (9x750 = 6750), hence 6750 mmHg.
  • 30 m/s = ...... km/hour → m/s to km/hour (30x3,6 = 108), hence 108 km/hour.
  • 7 feet = ...... inch → feet to inch (7x12 = 84), hence 84 inch.
  • 120 horsepower = ...... Watt → horsepower to Watt (120x746 = 89520), hence 89520 Watt.
  • 72 km/hour = ...... m/s → km/hour to m/s (72/3,6 = 20), hence 20 m/s.
  • 90 minute = ...... hour → minute to hour (90/60 = 1,5), hence 1,5 hour.
  • 1000 days = ...... year → days to years (1000/365,25 = 2,74), hence 2,74 years.
  • 13600 kg/m3 = ...... gram/cm3 → kg/m3 to gram/cm3 (13600/1000 = 13,6), hence 13,6 gram/cm3.
  • 800 Gauss = ...... Tesla → horsepower to Watt (800/104 = 0,02), hence 0,02 Tesla.

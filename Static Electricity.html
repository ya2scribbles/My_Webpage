Static Electricity

Concept of Electric Static

Try to rub your plastic ruler to your hair, after that move it toward scrap of paper. We could see scrap of paper would fly toward ruler. This is the example of static electricity. On daily activity level, each of object usually has neutral charge. The positive charge happened when object lose a few negative particle, while negative charge happened when object gain a few negative particle. When plastic ruler rub to your hair, it gains some negative particle. Hence it will attract scrap of paper whose more positive than ruler. That’s what happened in daily phenomenon. But on smaller level, it’s different story. And the negative particle that could freely move between object are known as electron.

We already establish that every matter in the world made by atoms. And atoms made by proton, neutrons, and electron. The composition of proton made element that we know in periodic table, the composition electron made ion that we know today, and composition of neutron made isotop of an element. Proton and neutron are the core of atoms, meanwhile electron orbit around the core of atoms. Proton has positive charge, electron has negative charge, and neutron has neutral. Hence in summary, information about component of atoms could be see in table below :

We established that different type of charge would attract of each other, meanwhile same type of charge would repel of each other. Meanwhile neutral object does not affect by charge. To understand why it’s happen, it’s because each charge has electric field. Electric field are field that would affect things around charge. And to make it easier to understand, we could draw electric field of charge like figure below :

As we could see from the figure, electric field for positive charge is outward meanwhile electric field of negative charge is inward. Hence explain why same charge are repelled to each other while different charge attracted to each other.

Figure 2 shows the direction of field between two charges when they interact with each other. As we can see, different signs of charge complement each other, hence they attract to each other. Meanwhile, same signs of charge have different directions, so they repel each other. Figure above shows us the property of interaction between two charges, but it doesn’t tell the magnitude of the electric field around a charge or the electric force between two charges. We will learn to calculate the electric field and force from a point of charge.

Field and Force of Charges

Every charge would influence every point around it. The influence at any point distance from charge happened because of electrostatic field of charge. Bigger charge would give bigger influence than smaller one. And more closer point to charge, more bigger influence of charge. This concept is known as field of electrostatic charge. The concept of field maybe could be understand through figure below :

In this figure, we could see that black point has same distance from charge A and B. However, field that black point feel is bigger on charge A than B because charge A is more bigger than B. Meanwhile for same charge, point p would feel effect of electrostatic field than point q. The reason is because point p is nearer than point q. Using this equation, we could find the value of field at any point around. Electrostatic field are vector quantity. That's why this equation only show magnitude of this quantity. To find direction, we could use figure 1.

If it positive charge, direction of field on that point are toward charge. Meanwhile, if it negative charge, direction of field on that point are outward charge. Hence, if one point get affected by more than one charge then first thing we need to do are determined the value and direction of each field at that point according to every charge. After that, we must used vector to calculate resultant of electrostatic charge at that point. When you put point of charge in that point, that point of charge would feel electrostatic force or Coulomb Force.

Coulomb force dictate that two charge would affect each other with same magnitude. The magnitude of this force would be proportional to value of each charge and square inverse with distance between two charge. Meanwhile for direction, it will attract to another charge if they have different sign, and repel each other if they have same sign.
